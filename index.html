01 · About

Physicist.
Artist. Explorer.

I'm a PhD researcher in experimental particle physics at DESY Hamburg, working within the CMS Collaboration at the LHC (CERN). My research is focused on long-lived particle (LLP) searches, specifically, studying the semileptonic decays of long-lived stau pairs using advanced machine learning techniques.

On the hardware side, I contribute to the CMS Phase-2 detector upgrade, assembling PS modules and conducting thermal tests on endcap module cooling materials. I'm also part of the Tau object group, where I've worked on tau fake rate measurements for UL2017 data.

Currently extending my stau LLP analysis to the Future Circular Collider (FCCee), and contributing to HEPData preservation for published CMS results. I hold a Masters in Physics from the University of Cologne (BCGS, grade 1.4) and a BSc (Hons) from the University of Delhi (9.676/10).

02 · Research & Contributions

Building the
instruments of discovery.

⭐ PhD Focus
Long-Lived Stau Pairs, CMS RUN 3 & FCCee

My primary research: searching for long-lived supersymmetric stau particles (τ̃) at the CMS experiment. I apply machine learning techniques, XGBoost, DNNs, Decision Trees, to distinguish long-lived stau decay signatures from SM backgrounds in the semileptonic channel. Currently extending this analysis to the Future Circular Collider (FCCee) to explore new sensitivity at higher energies. This work directly probes BSM physics and SUSY dark matter candidates.

Hardware
CMS Phase-2 Detector Upgrade

Assembly of PS (Pixel-Strip) modules for the CMS Inner Tracker upgrade. Conducting thermal tests on endcap module cooling materials in clean room environments, ensuring detector components meet strict operational requirements for HL-LHC.

Software · Analysis
Tau Fake Rate Measurements

Performed tau fake rate (FR) measurements for UL2017 (Ultra-Legacy) CMS data as part of the Tau Object Group. These calibration measurements are foundational for any analysis involving hadronic tau reconstruction across the collaboration.

Open Science
HEPData, Published CMS Results

Contributing to HEPData preservation and publication of a CMS paper, uploading analysis data, histograms, and supplementary material to the community's open-access high energy physics repository, enabling result reuse and reinterpretation.

Previous
ECAL Design, Lohengrin Light Dark Matter Search

Worked on the design of the Electron Calorimeter (ECAL) for the Lohengrin project, a light dark matter search experiment at the University of Bonn, under Dr. Matthias Hamer and Dr. Klaus Desch.
